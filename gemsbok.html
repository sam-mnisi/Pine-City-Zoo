GEMSBOK

Gemsbok are large antelopes of the genus Oryx, native to arid regions of Southern Africa, particularly the Kalahari Desert. The most well-known species is Oryx gazella, distinguished by its striking black-and-white facial markings and long, straight horns which both males and females possess. Gemsbok are superbly adapted to desert environments, capable of surviving high temperatures and limited water availability. They often get moisture from the plants they consume, such as wild melons and roots.
