MONKEY

“Monkey” is a broad term encompassing a wide variety of primate species, typically divided into Old World monkeys (Africa and Asia) and New World monkeys (Central and South America). Monkeys vary greatly in size, behavior, and habitat, but most are arboreal, social, and highly intelligent.

Common zoo species include capuchins, macaques, and howler monkeys. They often use vocalizations, facial expressions, and gestures for communication. Monkeys are omnivores, feeding on fruits, leaves, insects, and sometimes small animals.
