PANDA

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) is a bear native to south-central China. Known for their distinctive black-and-white coloring, pandas are primarily solitary and spend most of their time foraging for bamboo, which makes up over 99% of their diet.

Pandas have a special wrist bone that functions as a thumb, enabling them to grasp bamboo stalks. Despite being classified as carnivores, they have adapted to a herbivorous diet. Pandas are a global symbol of wildlife conservation due to their vulnerability and limited natural habitat.
